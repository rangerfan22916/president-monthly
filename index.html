John Quincy Adams

6th President of the United States (1825-1829)

Biography

John Quincy Adams was the 6th president of the United States and the son of John Adams. A skilled diplomat, he played a major role in foreign policy and later became a prominent member of the U.S. House of Representatives.

Major Achievements

  • Negotiated the Treaty of Ghent (1814), ending the War of 1812.
  • Authored the Monroe Doctrine as Secretary of State.
  • Advocated against slavery in Congress.
  • Supported scientific research and infrastructure projects.
