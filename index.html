John Quincy Adams

6th President of the United States (1825-1829)

Biography

John Quincy Adams, the eldest son of President John Adams, was born in 1767. He was a diplomat, senator, and secretary of state before becoming the sixth president of the United States. His early years were shaped by his father's role in the American Revolution.

Adams played a significant role in shaping U.S. foreign policy, negotiating key treaties and formulating the Monroe Doctrine. After his presidency, he served in the House of Representatives for 17 years, where he became a leading opponent of slavery.

Major Achievements

  • Negotiated the Treaty of Ghent (1814), ending the War of 1812.
  • Authored the Monroe Doctrine as Secretary of State.
  • Advocated against slavery in Congress and defended the Amistad captives.
  • Expanded U.S. infrastructure, supporting canals and roads.
  • Promoted scientific research, including the Smithsonian Institution.
